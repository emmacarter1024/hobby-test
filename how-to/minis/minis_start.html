What Do I Need?

To get started painting a D&D or other TTG mini you do not need a lot of fancy or expensive equipment. You can do most of the basics (and even some pretty advanced things) with just a brush, paint, knife and a few things you have lying around the house!

Brushes

You will need a brush or two to get started. To start off, you only need two - a standard brush for doing base coats and a detail brush for the details. You can find a great starter brush set on our store here.

Hobby Knife

When you pull some of the cheaper minis out the box for the first time, you'll find some of them have edges or minor printing defects that you'll have to tidy up. A cheap hobby knife is the best way to fix those fast before you start applying paint.

Slicing and Dicing

Most minis are made with crappy plastic so you have to cut off the burrs and other random defects. Trim them off, and try not to cut off a finger while you're doing it ok?
